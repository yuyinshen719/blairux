CommonWealth Magazine Group

How to better engage readers in the digital era?

In the summer of 2015 I was selected from a large pool of applicants for an internship at CommonWealth, one of the most prestigious Asian magazines known for its concern about humanistic and political-economic issues. As a UX design assistant I learned how traditional media outlets can use advanced technologies to enhance communication with their audiences. CommonWealth Magazine Group has huge numbers of events, workshops, and talks every month, yet bussiness units lack a platform to gather all participants' information and a convenient way to sell tickets or vouchers. Thus Digital Innovation Department decided to create a website as CommonWealth's events platform, and a way to interact with readers/customers/event participants.

My contribution

As the only UX design intern, I helped the department established UX design process. In less than 2 months, I was able to finish stakeholder interviews, competitive analysis, feature developing, low-fi prototyping, and user testing for the project.

About CommonWealth

Launched in June 1981, CommonWealth magazine is Taiwan's first political- economic magazine run by professional journalists. CommonWealth reports with an energetic, future-oriented, outward-looking attitude, attempting to define Taiwan's position amidst the rapidly changing trends that are shaping the world. CommonWealth serves as a bridge of communication between leading figures and the broader professional class, in pursuit of the magazine's foundational objective: a better society in Taiwan. CommonWealth Magazine has received over 100 major awards, both international and domestic, for its outstanding journalism.

Problems

  • The increase numbers of events or campaigns held in CommonWealth branches (CommonWealth, CommonHealth, Cheers, CommonWealth Parenting…)
  • The decrease number of attendance rate in the off-line workshops
  • Overlap of human resources during off-line events
  • Sharing profits with other event organizing platforms
  • There’s no centralized management of attendee lists
  • There’s no followup for event attendees - losing track of potential customers

Project scope

This event web is to increase the stickiness of our members and enlarge the customers base. By attending any of CommonWealth business unit's event, we can maintain the relationships even after the event. Existing customers can get more personalized information and discount by interacting with CommonWealth. Thus the web forms a virtuous circle.

Developing features

After understanding the needs from CommonWealth members and stakeholders, I organized the needs and translated into features for the website. Using a mind map allows stakeholders and project teammates to understand the needs and goals of this project.

User testing

I designed tasks especially for CommonWealth events that users would encounter during the process of ordering and distributing vouchers. The biggest challenge was that CommonWealth usually seperates workshops in different sessions, with different topics. That was different from most event sign-up services. Participants might join more than one session. Also, sometimes there are discounts for group order. We tested 4 order scenarios which are specific to CommonWealth's events, and tested by low-fi prototypes.
